Our Concerns

The proposed rezoning of Embry Farms with 1/4-acre lots threatens to urbanize Johns Creek's last rural area - a region rich in Cherokee heritage and designated for preservation in the 2018 Comprehensive Plan.

If approved as submitted, this proposal may harm all stakeholders:

  • The City risks infrastructure strain and diminished public trust
  • The Applicant risks a failed sales strategy due to impractical pricing and congestion
  • The Community risks decreased property values, increased traffic, and erosion of Shakerag's rural identity

Environmental Limitations

The Embry Farms site has significant topographical challenges that limit responsible development:

  • Minimal road frontage
  • Steep slopes and large floodplains
  • Over 73% of the land lies within the protected Chattahoochee River Corridor

For decades, the Embry family used this parcel as a private farm because of these environmental constraints. Any future development must respect these limitations.

Legal Concerns

We believe that:

  • The 2018 Comprehensive Plan clearly reduced allowable density in Shakerag to 1 unit per net acre or less
  • CUP zoning is being improperly used to bypass AG-1 1-acre minimum standards
  • The 2019 Ashton Woods denial should serve as binding precedent
  • Critical zoning provisions are being misinterpreted as merely "best practices" rather than enforceable requirements

Reference Documents:

  • 📄 Johns Creek Comprehensive Plan 2018-2028 (PDF)
  • 📄 City of Johns Creek Comprehensive Plan Official Page
  • 📄 Georgia DCA - Johns Creek Comprehensive Plan Resources

Our Request

We respectfully ask the City Council to:

  • Follow the Planning Commission's recommendation to reject the proposal
  • Review legal standards from the Georgia Planning Act, 2018 Comprehensive Plan, and Zoning Ordinance
  • Consider the 2019 Ashton Woods denial as binding precedent
  • Recognize the risk of setting a precedent that could erode the entire Shakerag Character Area

This is not anti-development. We support responsible growth that respects our community's character and environmental constraints.
